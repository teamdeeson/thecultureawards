2014 awards categories

Kent’s Cultural Icon Award

This Award chosen by public vote will be given to an individual with strong connections to Kent who people feel proud to have associated with Kent. Four eminent, popular and creative people have been shortlisted.

Sponsored by:

Some of the most dynamic people working in the arts and creative industries at a national and international level have a strong connection with the local region. As part of the 50th anniversary celebrations, the University of Kent is pleased to join with the local community to make an award which recognises the individual who has helped put Kent on the creative map. For 50 years, the University has made a major cultural contribution to the south east region. We have always placed great importance on not only providing outstanding teaching and research opportunities, but championing the arts in all its different guises.

Canterbury Award

Sponsored by:

A discretionary Award, given to an individual or organisation who is recognised for their special cultural achievements, and who is connected to East Kent. This award is not open to general entry. The nominees are selected by the judging panel.

Canterbury City Council has been a key partner in the Culture Awards since they began 4 years ago. The Council has a long standing tradition of supporting the arts, helping to develop and encourage activities, projects and initiatives that enhance the experience of Canterbury for residents and visitors alike. Through our support to artists who live or work in the district we create a fertile space for creativity and through our investment in facilities we maximise audience participation. The Cultural Awards are an important part of raising awareness of the artistic excellence this region enjoys and we are proud to be part of them.

Creative Entrepreneur

Sponsored by:

The judges will be looking for an individual who has achieved notable commercial results within a creative business or organisation in East Kent. Businesses or artists do not have to be new but do have to be able to demonstrate how their creative business has generated income in 2013 in a notably entrepreneurial way.

East Kent is steeped in history and culture exists in every corner. From museums and galleries to thriving festivals and theatre productions, we can all recognise culture that shapes this region. The Culture Awards celebrate achievements of businesses and individuals from the previous year across the region, which is why Barretts are delighted to be a sponsor of the Creative Entrepreneur 2014 award. Culture is continually changing and developing and this annual event allows businesses to join together, support and embrace the culture that makes East Kent a great place to love and enjoy.

Experience Award

Awarded to “the best cultural experience” and judged on a range of factors from audience feedback to quality and innovation.

Sponsored by:

Our mission is “to be the best at teaching & learning” and for our students to achieve world-class results. Situated in the heart of East Kent, Canterbury College was established in 1947 and has grown to be one of the largest Further and Higher Education colleges in the area. With almost 10,000 students studying around 500 programmes, we’ve created an exciting environment where people of all ages, backgrounds and abilities can meet, learn and grow. Our brand-new state-of-the-art campus combined with excellent academic and student support will help you to achieve your ambitions.

Digital Award

This new award will be given to a company or person using digital or emerging technologies innovatively to create an outstanding result.

Sponsored by:

Deeson Creative is proud to support the Culture Awards as they highlight excellence in the creative sector, particularly in areas close to our heart such as digital. We have the privilege through our technical and creative community events such as Digibury to witness how vibrant the sector is locally and look forward to seeing what’s next! We know the best results emerge when we combine the talents of a multi-disciplinary team; the variety in this year’s awards is a great demonstration of this.

The Award will be given to a marketing campaign undertaken by a by creative business, agency or individual. In addition to outstanding creativity the judges will be looking for evidence of the campaigns impact on the target audience and evidence of how the campaign supports and contributes to a clear brand message.

Cultural Champion

Awarded to a local business or individual from the business community, who has actively supported/ significantly contributed to a cultural event, activity or organisation within the boundary of East Kent. Individuals or organisations may nominate themselves or can be nominated by a third party.

Sponsored by:

Canterbury Christ Church University, venue host for the launch of the Culture Awards 2014, have been involved with the Culture Awards since 2011 and we are again proud to be a sponsor of an award. The Culture Awards highlight individual and collective achievements of the Canterbury district and East Kent and this year we are delighted to sponsor the Cultural Champion Award.

BEST PROJECT INVOLVING CHILDREN AND YOUNG PEOPLE

Awarded to group or individual whose cultural activity has made the most positive impact on children and Young People within the East Kent region. The judges will be looking for evidence of immediate feedback and sustainability.

BEST PROJECT INVOLVING THE WIDER COMMUNITY

This Award, for an organisation, group or individual who has reached outside traditional arts audiences to actively involve the wider community.

Sponsored by:

The University for the Creative Arts (UCA) is pleased to sponsor ‘The best project involving the wider community’ category within the Culture Awards. Working with the local creative and cultural community is at the heart of what we do at UCA. Through its Intercultural Culture-led Regeneration project (ICR), UCA funds the East Kent Cultural Conversations (EKCC) programme where we work closely with cultural organisations to provide placement and project opportunities for UCA students and graduates. EKCC provides forums for discussion and collaboration where cultural organisations promote their projects and invite artists in East Kent to participate.

DESTINATION EAST KENT AWARD (previously Destination Canterbury)

This Award will be given to an organisation, group or individual who has demonstrably raised the profile of East Kent’s cultural offering regionally, nationally and internationally. This allows work taking place outside Kent to be included so long as it is presented by a Kent-based organisation.

Sponsored by:

Abode is delighted to be supporting the prestigious Culture Awards again this year, by sponsoring Destination East Kent. East Kent as a destination is one of the most important market segments to the hotel and is a key driver to our success and many other businesses.

ONE TO WATCH AWARD

All about outstanding potential and the promise of realisation, this Award is for cultural organisations, groups or individuals in East Kent that started out less than 5 years ago.

Sponsored by:

As Canterbury's foremost interior design store, Lenley’s has always enjoyed a strong connection with local artists. According to MD Jonathan Watts, "Lenley’s business is all about selecting the most innovative and timeless design ideas to help inspire our customers in the choices they make for their home. We travel across Europe & the UK in this pursuit, so what's really wonderful about the 'One to Watch' awards is it provides inspiration for us on our doorstep."

EAST KENT PEOPLE’S AWARD

Awarded to a cultural organisation in East Kent which can demonstrate engagement with its local community, popularity across the region and continuing efforts in developing existing and new audiences. Entrants will be shortlisted by the judging panel and the eventual winner decided (from the shortlist) by public vote.

Sponsored by:

The people who live, work and play in this area are at the heart of what we do at the KM Group. Our newspapers, our KentOnline website and our kmfm radio stations are all proud to be sponsoring this award not least because it will be chosen by the public. There is so much to celebrate when it comes to Kent’s artistic, cultural and creative industries and we can't wait to do what we can to help spread the word because working together, we make a difference.

ARTIST AWARD

Specifically for a new work of art or performance judged to be artistically excellent and open to single practitioners or collaborative groups.

Sponsored by:

The University for the Creative Arts (UCA) is pleased to sponsor the ‘Artist’ category within the Culture Awards. UCA offers a range of courses in art, architecture, design, fashion, communications, graphics and media across its campuses in the South East of England. See www.ucreative.ac.uk. Many UCA graduates become practicing artists in East Kent, contributing positively to the cultural and creative community. UCA offers a range of creative expertise and technical services to support local artists, including access to specialist equipment such as the bronze foundry and glassworks as well as cutting-edge 3d and digital textile printing facilities.
